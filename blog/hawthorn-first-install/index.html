Our First Install: A Melbourne Family Took Their Gas Boiler Off the Wall. Here's What Happened.

Hawthorn, Victoria. Early May 2026. Real home, real bills, real numbers from the customer's own app, live since day one.

For five years, Mike and Kay wanted to get off gas.

They have a hydronic home in Hawthorn, radiators, the kind of even, quiet warmth that gas hydronic does well and that most heat pump retrofits struggle to match. They knew the gas boiler's days were numbered. They'd watched the bills climb. They'd looked at the alternatives more than once.

And every time, the same wall: a traditional hydronic heat pump replacement in Australia runs $20,000 to $40,000. For a like-for-like comfort upgrade, that's a hard number to justify.

In their own words, the week we took the gas meter off the wall:

"We've been looking for 5 years for a hydronic heat pump, but we didn't want to pay $25,000. We finally did it."

Mike & Kay, Hawthorn

In early May, their gas boiler came off the wall. The gas meter came off the house entirely. And they became the first Australian home to run a gas hydronic system converted to a Thermal Dawn heat pump with thermal storage.

This is what’s happened since.

What we actually installed

We replaced the gas boiler with a Thermal Dawn system: a reverse-cycle hydronic heat pump paired with a 35 kWh thermal store: an Australian-made, heavily insulated tank that holds heat as hot water until the house needs it.

The radiators stayed. The pipework stayed. That's the point. We didn't rip the house apart to switch fuel. (If you're wondering whether your own radiators are compatible with a heat pump, that's the single most common question we get, and we wrote a full explainer on flow temperatures and radiator compatibility.)

The behaviour change is the interesting part. A gas boiler burns fuel the moment you want heat. Thermal Dawn does it on a different clock:

  • It charges the thermal store during the day, when Mike and Kay's rooftop solar is generating, plus a 3-hour free-electricity window their retailer offers.
  • Then it heats the house off stored energy through the evening and overnight: the peak hours when grid power is most expensive.

As Mike put it, watching his own dashboard: the grid barely sees it.

The moment that surprised us

We brought in a licensed plumbing partner, Hub-Air, to run the install with us. One of the installers on site, Dale, had spent his career on gas. He was, politely, a sceptic about heat pumps for hydronic.

By the end of the day he'd asked to do the next job.

That mattered more to us than the bill savings, because it points at something bigger than one house. The reason gas installers stay off heat pumps isn't loyalty to gas, it's that heat pump hydronic has historically been fiddly to spec and size. We pre-configure the system so the technical risk is taken out before anyone arrives on site. Take that barrier away and even a gas lifer wants in.
