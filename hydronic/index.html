A Standard Heat Pump Still Leaves You Exposed

Swapping a gas boiler for a standard hydronic heat pump is a step forward. But it doesn't solve the fundamental problem: your home needs heat most at dawn and dusk, exactly when electricity prices peak.

A standard heat pump draws from the grid during those expensive morning and evening hours. You've escaped gas, but you're still paying premium rates every time the temperature drops at night.

Thermal Dawn is different. Our system charges during the day on your solar, storing thermal energy while the sun shines and rates are low, then delivers that stored warmth through your radiators and underfloor heating all evening, without touching the grid.

Why Not Just a Battery?

It's the first question almost everyone asks. The honest answer: we're not anti-battery, we're pro-economics. For the loads that dominate your bill, heating, cooling, and hot water, storing energy as heat is far cheaper than storing it as electricity.

Home battery (lithium) Thermal Dawn
Stores Electricity Heat & cooling, directly
Cost to store your thermal load High, you pay for premium battery capacity A fraction of the cost
Conversion losses Electricity → heat, lossy at every step Solar → heat, stored as heat
Degradation Fades within ~10 years No charge-cycle limit, lasts decades
Fire risk Yes None
Made in Mostly imported Australia

A battery is a fine way to run your lights and power points. But heating and hot water are where the big, predictable load sits, and that's exactly what thermal storage does best, for less. Some homes end up with both; if you're choosing one first, this is the one that pays back your heating bill.

Frequently asked questions

How is thermal storage different from a battery?

Lithium batteries store electricity, then convert it back to heat when you need it, losing efficiency at every step. Thermal Dawn stores heat and cold directly, so your dawn solar becomes evening comfort without the conversion losses, degradation, or fire risk. It's infrastructure that lasts decades, not a battery that fades in 10 years. (See "Why not just a battery?" above for the full comparison.)

How much does it cost?

Our target is around half the cost of a comparable lithium battery system for heating, cooling, and hot water, with no degradation and no replacement costs in 10 years. Total installed cost depends on your home size, existing pipework, and electrical setup. Book a free, no-obligation site assessment for a fixed quote.

How much will I save?

Based on modelling in real Australian homes, typical customers save $800–$1,500/yr per year by shifting thermal loads away from peak grid prices. Your savings depend on your climate zone, home size, energy tariffs, and solar generation. We'll run a personalised estimate during your site assessment, most systems pay for themselves in 3–5 years.

Do I need solar panels already?

No, but you'll maximise savings with solar. Without panels, Thermal Dawn can still charge during off-peak tariff windows and deliver heating, cooling, and hot water during expensive peak times. Adding solar later? The system integrates seamlessly.

What's included in the system?

Thermal Dawn Core (thermal store), high-efficiency heat pump (heating, cooling, hot water integration), optional domestic hot water module, smart controls + app, remote monitoring & OTA updates (opt-in). All components backed by comprehensive warranty coverage.
